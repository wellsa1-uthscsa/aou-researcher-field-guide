# The All of Us Researcher Field Guide
## Project Charter
**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Status:** Adopted  
**Effective Date:** July 17, 2026

# Executive Summary

The **All of Us Researcher Field Guide** is an open educational resource dedicated to teaching rigorous, reproducible biomedical research using the All of Us Researcher Workbench. The guide provides practical instruction in electronic health record (EHR) analysis, genomics, wearable sensor data, epidemiology, statistics, and scientific computing through complete analytical workflows derived from real research projects.

Unlike traditional software documentation, the Field Guide focuses on how researchers think. Every chapter explains not only how to perform an analysis, but why each methodological decision was made, how results should be validated, and how workflows can be adapted to new scientific questions.

# Vision

To become the definitive educational resource for investigators using the All of Us Researcher Workbench by making population-scale biomedical research approachable, transparent, reproducible, and scientifically rigorous.

# Mission

To teach researchers how to transform raw All of Us data into reproducible scientific discoveries through thoughtful study design, validated analytical workflows, and practical implementation.

# Guiding Principles

1. Teach scientific thinking before software.
2. Explain why before how.
3. Build from real research.
4. Reproducibility is a scientific requirement.
5. Validation is part of every workflow.
6. Transparency over perfection.
7. One page, one question.
8. Practical before comprehensive.
9. Consistency creates clarity.
10. Build a community resource.

# The Field Guide Method

Every complete analytical workflow should be understandable through the following framework:

| Stage | Guiding Question |
|--------|------------------|
| Question | What scientific question are we trying to answer? |
| Study Design | What population and design best answer that question? |
| Cohort Construction | Which participants should be included, and why? |
| Phenotype Engineering | How do we translate clinical or biological concepts into analyzable variables? |
| Data Engineering | How are data integrated, cleaned, transformed, and validated? |
| Statistical Analysis | What analytical approach best addresses the question? |
| Validation | How do we know the results are robust and reproducible? |
| Interpretation | What conclusions are supported by the data? |
| Communication | How should findings be communicated so others can reproduce and extend the work? |

This framework is the intellectual backbone of the Field Guide. Every major pipeline should reinforce these stages regardless of research domain.

# Educational Philosophy

Every page should answer:

1. What is this concept?
2. Why does it matter?
3. How is it implemented?
4. How do I know it worked?
5. What should I do next?

# North Star

**Every page should leave the reader more capable of conducting rigorous, reproducible biomedical research than they were before they opened it.**

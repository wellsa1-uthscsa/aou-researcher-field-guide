# The All of Us Researcher Field Guide

# Brand Identity Specification

**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Approved (Founding Brand)  
**Effective Date:** July 17, 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the official visual and editorial identity of the All of Us Researcher Field Guide. It serves as the single source of truth for branding decisions and complements (but does not replace) the Design System.

---

# Brand Mission

**A comprehensive field guide for rigorous, reproducible biomedical research using the All of Us Researcher Workbench.**

---

# Brand Statement

**The All of Us Researcher Field Guide helps researchers build rigorous, reproducible analyses by teaching the principles behind every step of the research process.**

---

# Tagline

**Practical methods for reproducible biomedical research.**

# Audience

Primary:

- All of Us researchers
- Biomedical scientists
- Clinician-investigators
- Graduate students
- Physician-scientists

Secondary:

- Students learning reproducible biomedical data science
- Collaborators seeking workflow references

---

# Core Brand Principles

1. Teach scientific reasoning before software.
2. Explain why before how.
3. Prioritize reproducibility.
4. Prefer clarity over completeness.
5. Build confidence without oversimplifying.

---

# Visual Identity

The design language is **Modern Scientific**.

Primary inspirations:

- Modern academic publishing
- Apple Human Interface Guidelines
- Nature Reviews
- Stripe Documentation

Avoid:

- Dashboard aesthetics
- Corporate SaaS styling
- Government portals
- Decorative scientific imagery

---

# Logo Philosophy

The logo represents **scientific decision making** rather than biology or technology.

### Primary mark

A branching pathway symbol representing:

- Research questions
- Decision points
- Alternative analytical paths
- Evidence-based workflows

The branch-point motif should become the recurring visual language across the website.

---

# Illustration Philosophy

Illustrations exist to **teach**, not decorate.

Preferred illustrations include:

- Workflow diagrams
- Branching analytical pathways
- Cohort construction diagrams
- Study timelines
- Concept maps
- Validation workflows

## Homepage Rule

The homepage **must not** use screenshots or reproductions of the All of Us Researcher Workbench.

Instead, use original vector illustrations and abstract workflow graphics.

---

# Color Philosophy

Use the approved palette from the Design System.

Approximate visual balance:

- 70% White / Light Gray
- 20% Navy
- 8% Blue
- 2% Gold

Gold is reserved for emphasis and should never dominate the interface.

---

# Typography

Headings:
- Merriweather

Body:
- Inter

Code:
- JetBrains Mono

Typography should reinforce readability and long-form learning.

---

# UI Language

All interface components should communicate calm, precision, and approachability.

Use:

- Soft 10–12 px corner radius
- Thin borders
- Generous whitespace
- Subtle shadows
- Gentle hover elevation

Avoid sharp edges or visually aggressive components.

---

# Motion

Permitted:

- Fade
- Gentle lift
- Soft shadow transitions

Avoid decorative animations.

---

# Accessibility

Brand choices must support:

- High contrast
- Keyboard navigation
- Readable typography
- Meaningful alt text
- Color-independent communication

---

# Future Brand Assets

The official brand package will include:

- Primary logo

- Favicon
- Brand board
- SVG illustration library
- Homepage mockup

---

# Success Criteria

The brand is successful when a visitor immediately perceives the Field Guide as:

- scientifically rigorous,
- approachable,
- visually cohesive,
- modern,
- and focused on teaching reproducible biomedical research.
